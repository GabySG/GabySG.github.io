SecondBuy

Help Georgia Tech students to Buy & Sell used bikes

  Objective

Buyers choose a high quality-price-ratio bike quickly

Sellers avoid spending lots of time looking for buyers



 My role

I participated throughout the research process of contextual inquiry, interviews, survey and user evaluation.

Sep.2017 - December.2017
My awesome teamates
Lois, Angela and Akhil

  Approach

We conducted semi-structured interviews, contextual inquiries, and distribute a survey to identify problems that used-bike buyers and sellers meet. Based on the data gathered, we did affinity diagram to categorize collected data, whose findings are used to develop user personas.

These processes helped us understand our users better, and we identified 6 problems in their buying and selling experience of second-hand bikes. Considering their user scale and influence, we converged to 2 problems. Then we organized divergent brainstorming design sessions with 19 divergent design ideas. After doing a thorough analysis of their feasibility, creativity and to the biggest extent they can solve 2 main problems, we then converged to 2 design ideas.

The 2 ideas were presented to our class peers for feedback and evaluation by storyboards, wireframes. After multiple iterations, we finally came up with a system called SecondBuy with a mobile app and a physical bike station.

Personas

We had a starting point of more than 5 personas before we began. We created 3 person types we felt we needed considering different age, gender and cultural backgourds: 2 buyer personas and 1 seller persona. Secondary personas about expert users can be created in the future.

Hi-Fi prototype after Cognitive Walkthrough


We decided on the Mobile app since nowadays people especially the young have been using cellphone which has advantage in size and acccessibility.





And then I quickly made a Lo-Fi interaction prototype and we showed it to experts for cognitive walkthourgh when they give us advices about what problems they think customers might encounter within UI design, task flow and details of features.



We moved to a Hi-Fi interaction prototype based on these feedbacks.

Usability test


We present the Hi-Fi prorotype to 8 potential users, 3 of them are potential sellers, 5 are buyers to do usability test.





We have three parts in our usability testing: Tasks, post-each-task rating, post-all-task SUS and open-ended questions.
