Invisible Cities

An AR app that collaborates multiplayer's expansive artworks in public areas.

  Objective

InvisibleCities, funded by Arts @ Tech, aims at offering a platform people express their opinions; a recreation method to have fun; a place where they can freely uncover creativity.

Augmented Reality (AR) is a conceptual leap to design content that "lives" outside the phone in the real campus.

  My Role

I am leading a nine-people UX team, starting User Experience research from scratch, did interviews, competitive analysis. Every teammember was highly involved when coming up with affinity diagram and personas. Then we sat down together, came up with design ideas. In design, I guided hand sketch, lo-fi and was mainly responsible for hi-fi.

  Time&Team

Invisible Cities has been mainly designed from January to present with my awesome teammates: nine undergraduates and Joshua, a PhD candidate at GeorgiaTech.

We are working on 2 publications after receiving all-around positive feedback from stakeholders and students during a campus-wide presentation. This game will be published on April ideally.


  Process

EMPATHIZE

  Interview


We conducted 4 structured interviews when we invite interviewees to retrospect recent experience of playing a VR/AR game. These interviews helped us to know what problems are suffering from current VR/AR devices, their movivations of using these devices and what they expect in a virtual world.

22 unstructured interview are also conducted, more like a focus group where people speak freely about their arts related experience, from which we want to know the reasons behind their painting, potential influences about their works.

  Competitive Analysis

In order to get a good feeling about what current AR apps look like, three of the team are responsible for looking for one block app, which is similar to ours and one popular AR map separately. After exploration of these apps, researchers need to take a note of what they like and dislike the most.



Researchers found that people like apps for specific reasons: 4 out of 7 are described as "creative", same for "simple UI".

In terms of dislikes, 2 apps seem to have "delay" and "rendering", sometime "crash down" issue due to real-time interactivity. 3 apps have to add "ads" to balance their own business model but failed to impress users. 4 apps put users in an unknown zone without any "guidance, tutorial or help files", which is required especially when "UI elements are not intuititve" to users.

  Contextual Inquiry

Horace is a painting ecstatic majoring in biology at University of Georgia. I went there for contextual inquiry.

Before calling it a day with his creative work, a slogan "计划生育,人人有责" (Everybody is responsible to control baby birth), prevalent in 1990s in China, was added.

Surprised, I asked why. "I'm an international student, I have been studying here alone for 6 years, sometimes ideas would just pop up in my mind, most of them are related with my childhood experience", He replied, "Besides, it's sarcastic that this popular 'one-child' policy has been cancelled years ago."

  Affinity Diagram

By collecting data from interviews, contextual inquiries and surveys, we began to do affinity mapping to categorize our findings.

Learning from the affinity diagram, we decided to move on with our AR dashboard with two main functions: Explore, where users can interact with the real environment and others' works in the location where users are; Build: where people can build their own statue, besides, simple UI and enough customization options, e.g. colors, textures, templates should be offered.

Besides, users can get access to user profile and see one person's all works. User should be able to edit their own works without location limits. Clear tutorial should be offered in the beginning and feedbacks should also be given clearly.
