Invisible Cities

An AR app that collaborates multiplayer's expansive artworks in public areas.

  Objective

InvisibleCities aims at offering a platform to express people's voice, a recreation method to take some steam off and have fun, a place where people can uncover creativity.

AR (Augmented Reality) is a conceptual leap to design content that "lives" outside the phone in the real campus, even though user can only see it and intearat with it via the smartphone class.

  My Role

I lead a nine-people UX team, starting User Experience research from scratch, did unstructured and structured interviews, Competitive analysis. Every teammember was highly involved when coming up with affinity diagram and personas. Then we sat down together, came up with design ideas. In design, I guided hand sketch, lo-fi and was mainly responsible for hi-fi. Currently I am leading the team to make UI in Unity and link design with scripts.

  Time&Team

Invisible Cities was mainly implemented from January to present with my awesome teammates: Joshua, who is a PhD candidate at GeorgiaTech and has been taken charge of the back-end delicately.

Our team is planning to have 2 publications based on our idea and User experience method. This game will be supposed to be published on April.


  Approach

We distributed conducted unstructured and structured interviews with people with or without VR and AR experiences. Based on the data gathered, we explored more than 10 AR theme games, and take a note of what we like and dislike, then share with the team. To get un-biased opinions, we also went through customer reviews from Apple store. Later we built affinity diagram to categorize collected data, whose findings are used to develop user personas.

Personas helped us to understand we are making product for someone, not everyone. Later we identified problems and functions users value the most in their experience of using an AR app that creates art. During the brainstorming session, we came up with dozens of ideas based on users' needs. we worked closely with back-end team, so that every function was added a tag: "Difficulty level" besides it to indicate how hard it is to embrace it in version1. After doing a thorough analysis of their feasibility, creativity, we decided to move on with X ideas.

Design brainstorming begins when ideas are settled down. Everyone's design ideas were presented to peers on whiteboard for feedback. We then moved on with the most popular design idea. Later wireframe, color palette were also confirmed by most teammembers' consensus. After multiple iterations of design and feedback, we finally came up with the final design.


  Process

  Interview


We conducted 4 structured interviews when we invite interviewees to retrospect recent experience of playing a VR/AR game. These interviews helped us to know what problems are suffering from current VR/AR devices, their movivations of using these devices and what they expect in a virtual world.

22 unstructured interview are also conducted, more like a focus group where people speak freely about their arts related experience, from which we want to know the reasons behind their painting, potential influences about their works. Data we gathered turned out to be very helpful in the upcoming Define section

  Competitive Analysis

In order to get a good feeling about what current AR apps look like, three of the team are responsible for looking for one block app, which is similar to ours and one popular AR map separately. After exploration of these apps, researchers need to take a note of what they like and dislike the most.



Researchers found that people like apps for specific reasons: 4 out of 7 are described as "creative", same for "simple UI".

In terms of dislikes, 2 apps seems to have "delay" and "rendering" issue due to real-time interactivity. 3 apps have to add "ads" to balance their own business model but failed to impress users. 4 apps put users in an unknown zone without any "guidance, tutorial or help files", which is required especially when "UI elements are not intuititve" to users.
